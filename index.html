What is a producer platform?

What is a producer platform?

A producer platform is a technology-enabled business that serves producers: professionals whose clients follow them personally, not the brand they work under. Think wealth advisors, real estate agents, insurance brokers, med spa operators, and private jet brokers. Producer platforms give these professionals mechanical advantage by combining software and services into a single integrated platform, enabling them to grow faster while spending less time on operations. Unlike pure SaaS or traditional marketplaces, producer platforms participate in the economics of the producer's business, creating a fundamentally different and more durable business model.

How are producer platforms different from SaaS or marketplaces?

Producer platforms are a distinct business model that VCs and analysts frequently misclassify. They are not SaaS because they do more than sell software subscriptions: they deliver services, handle operations, and participate in the producer's revenue. They are not marketplaces because the demand comes with the producer, so there is no traditional chicken-and-egg problem. And they are not PE rollups because they do not acquire the producer's practice. Instead, producer platforms sit underneath the producer, providing the infrastructure that lets them be more productive while the platform captures margin from the supply side.

What is the producer economy?

The producer economy refers to the $6 trillion segment of the US economy, roughly 20% of GDP, that is driven by professionals whose clients follow them personally. This includes over 30 categories of professionals across wealth management, real estate, insurance, healthcare, aesthetics, private aviation, travel, and many more industries. These producers control their own client relationships and revenue, regardless of which firm or brand they operate under. The producer economy has historically been underserved by technology because the needs of producers do not fit neatly into existing SaaS or marketplace models.

What are examples of producer platforms?

The book profiles producer platforms across multiple industries. In wealth management, Farther is an all-in-one platform for wealth advisors that built its own asset manager. In real estate, Place provides holistic tech and services for top-producing real estate teams. In med spa and aesthetics, Moxie makes it easier to launch and grow a med spa. In private aviation, Goodwin built the first digital, instant marketplace for chartered flights. Other companies covered include Spark Advisors, HealthSherpa, Nayya, Thinksurance, PolicyFly, and Tern across insurance, benefits, and travel.
